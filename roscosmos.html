ROSCOSMOS - Russian Federal Space Agency

History & Overview

ROSCOSMOS is the governmental body responsible for the space science program of the Russian Federation and general aerospace research. Established in 1992 following the dissolution of the Soviet Union, it continues the legacy of the Soviet space program that launched Sputnik and sent Yuri Gagarin into space.

Famous Rockets

The Soyuz and Proton rockets are some of the most reliable and frequently used launch vehicles in history. Soyuz has transported astronauts to the ISS for decades and remains a cornerstone of human spaceflight.

Satellites

ROSCOSMOS has launched numerous satellites including navigation systems like GLONASS, weather satellites like Elektro-L, and Earth-observation satellites for remote sensing and defense.

Involvement with ISS

ROSCOSMOS is a founding partner of the ISS. It manages modules like Zvezda and Zarya, and provides crew transport using Soyuz and Progress spacecraft.

Major Missions

Key missions include Luna moon exploration program, Venera missions to Venus, the ongoing ExoMars collaboration with ESA, and upcoming missions like Luna 25 and Oryol.

Astronauts

Russian cosmonauts have played vital roles in space exploration history. Famous cosmonauts include Yuri Gagarin (first human in space), Valentina Tereshkova (first woman in space), and Gennady Padalka (record holder for most time spent in space).

International Collaborations

ROSCOSMOS collaborates with NASA, ESA, and other global partners. It is a core member of ISS operations and has previously partnered with India (ISRO), China (CNSA), and Europe (ESA) on lunar and Mars exploration missions.

Internships & Student Opportunities

ROSCOSMOS offers student outreach and technical internships through affiliated research institutions and universities in Russia. International students can sometimes participate via bilateral space agreements or exchange programs.
