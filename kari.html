Korea Aerospace Research Institute (KARI)

History & Overview

The Korea Aerospace Research Institute (KARI) was established in 1989 and is South Korea's national aerospace agency. KARI is responsible for the development of satellites, rockets, and space exploration technologies, playing a key role in advancing South Korea's space capabilities.

Famous Rockets

KARI is known for developing the NARO rocket and the KSLV series, which marks South Korea's venture into the field of space exploration. KARI has been instrumental in enhancing South Korea's space launch capabilities.

Satellites

KARI has developed several satellites, including the KOMPSAT series for Earth observation. The institute is also engaged in future satellite development programs for scientific research and telecommunications.

Involvement with ISS

While KARI does not directly manage ISS operations, it is involved in joint research projects with international space agencies, contributing to the development of technologies that are used in collaboration with the ISS.

Major Missions

KARI has been involved in several key missions, such as the launch of the first South Korean satellite, KITSAT-1, and the launch of KOMPSAT-1, -2, and -3. They have also participated in international space missions through collaborations with NASA, ESA, and others.

Astronauts

South Korea's first astronaut, Yi So-yeon, flew to space aboard Soyuz TMA-12 in 2008. KARI continues to support space exploration efforts by providing research and technology development for future space missions.

International Collaborations

KARI collaborates with various international space agencies like NASA, ESA, and ISRO, sharing knowledge and technology for satellite development and launch systems. Their international partnerships are critical for advancing South Korea's space agenda.

Internships & Student Opportunities

KARI offers internships for students in aerospace engineering and related fields. The institute provides opportunities for young researchers to contribute to space missions and the development of future technologies.
