The Eyes and Ears in Space

üîπ What is a Satellite?

A satellite is any object that orbits another object in space. Artificial satellites are human-made machines placed in orbit around Earth or other celestial bodies to perform various functions.

üîπ Types of Satellites

  • Communication Satellites: Enable global telecommunications and broadcasting (e.g., Intelsat, INSAT)
  • Weather Satellites: Monitor atmospheric conditions and climate (e.g., NOAA, Meteosat)
  • Navigation Satellites: Provide positioning data globally (e.g., GPS, GLONASS, Galileo)
  • Scientific Satellites: Conduct scientific research and space observations (e.g., Hubble Space Telescope)
  • Spy Satellites: Used for reconnaissance and national security

  • Polar Orbit: Passes over Earth's poles, useful for global coverage

üîπ Key Components of a Satellite

  • Power System (solar panels, batteries)
  • Communication System (antennas, transponders)
  • Payload (cameras, sensors, instruments)
  • Propulsion System (for orbit adjustments)
  • Thermal Control System

Satellites play a crucial role in global communications, navigation, weather forecasting, scientific research, Earth observation, and military intelligence.
