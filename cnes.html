CNES - French Space Agency

History & Overview

CNES (Centre National d'Études Spatiales) is the French government space agency, established in 1961. It is responsible for shaping and implementing France’s space policy in Europe and supports both civilian and military space efforts.

Famous Rockets

CNES developed the Diamant rocket and has been a key partner in the Ariane series of rockets with ESA and Arianespace. Ariane 5 and Ariane 6 are major European launch vehicles supported by CNES expertise.

Satellites

CNES operates Earth observation satellites like SPOT, Helios, and SWOT, which monitor the environment, climate, and support defense and disaster response missions. It also works on scientific satellites like COROT and MICROSCOPE.

Involvement with ISS

France contributes to the ISS through ESA. French astronauts regularly participate in missions to the space station, and CNES supports European experiments and modules on board.

Major Missions

CNES has collaborated on landmark missions like Mars Express, Rosetta, and Philae (comet landing). It also partners with NASA on oceanography satellites like Jason-3 and SWOT.

Astronauts

French astronauts include Thomas Pesquet, Jean-François Clervoy, and Claudie Haigneré. Thomas Pesquet flew aboard SpaceX Crew-2 and served aboard the ISS multiple times.

International Collaborations

CNES works closely with ESA, NASA, ISRO, and other space agencies. It contributes to many global missions, shares data with international partners, and helps advance European space ambitions.

Internships & Student Opportunities

CNES offers internships to students through its education portal, supporting engineering, physics, and space science. Collaborations with French universities and ESA offer young researchers hands-on experience.
