JAXA - Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency

History & Overview

JAXA was formed in 2003 through the merger of three Japanese aerospace institutions. It leads Japan’s efforts in space science, satellite development, planetary exploration, and international cooperation in space missions.

Famous Rockets

JAXA's main rockets include the H-IIA and H-IIB series, and the new H3 rocket. These launch satellites, planetary probes, and cargo to the ISS. Japan also developed the Epsilon rocket for small payloads.

Satellites

JAXA operates weather, Earth observation, and communication satellites such as Himawari, GOSAT, and ALOS. These contribute to disaster management, environmental monitoring, and scientific research.

Involvement with ISS

Japan is a key partner of the ISS program. JAXA contributed the Kibo laboratory module and launches HTV cargo resupply missions to support astronauts and research aboard the station.

Major Missions

JAXA has conducted groundbreaking missions like Hayabusa and Hayabusa2 asteroid sample return missions, Akatsuki to Venus, and MMX (Martian Moons eXploration). It is also involved in future lunar missions with NASA.

Astronauts

JAXA has produced several astronauts including Koichi Wakata (commander on ISS), Soichi Noguchi, and Naoko Yamazaki. They represent Japan in international missions aboard the ISS.

International Collaborations

JAXA collaborates with NASA, ESA, CNSA, and ISRO. It participates in the Artemis program, Earth science missions with NASA, and Moon rover development with Toyota and other partners.

Internships & Student Opportunities

JAXA offers programs for students and researchers through JAXA's Space Education Center and collaboration with universities. International students can apply through JICA, JST, or special research internships.
