NASA - National Aeronautics and Space Administration

History & Overview

NASA was established in 1958 by the United States government, following the launch of Sputnik by the Soviet Union. Its mission is to pioneer the future in space exploration, scientific discovery, and aeronautics research. Over the decades, NASA has led humanity’s greatest space missions and scientific achievements, such as landing a man on the Moon in 1969.

Famous Rockets

NASA developed iconic rockets like the Saturn V, which launched the Apollo missions, and the Space Launch System (SLS), designed for future deep-space exploration including Artemis missions.

Satellites

NASA operates advanced satellites like Hubble Space Telescope, Terra, and Landsat to observe Earth, the cosmos, and collect crucial environmental data.

Involvement with ISS

NASA is a key contributor to the International Space Station (ISS), working with global partners like ESA, Roscosmos, and JAXA. It launches astronauts, provides supplies, and conducts experiments in low Earth orbit aboard the ISS.

Major Missions

NASA has spearheaded missions like Apollo (Moon), Voyager (interstellar space), Mars Curiosity & Perseverance (rovers), Artemis (return to Moon), and James Webb Space Telescope (deep space observation).

Astronauts

NASA has trained and launched hundreds of astronauts, including legends like Neil Armstrong, Sally Ride (first American woman in space), and Sunita Williams (Indian-American astronaut).

International Collaborations

NASA collaborates globally with agencies like ESA (Europe), Roscosmos (Russia), JAXA (Japan), ISRO (India), and CSA (Canada). These partnerships drive forward missions, research, and development of cutting-edge technologies for peaceful space exploration.

Internships & Student Opportunities

NASA offers internship programs for students from high school to graduate levels in fields like engineering, astrophysics, AI, and robotics. Global students can participate via the NASA I2 and LPI (Lunar and Planetary Institute) internships. These programs aim to inspire the next generation of space explorers.
