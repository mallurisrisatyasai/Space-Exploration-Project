Virgin Galactic

History & Overview

Virgin Galactic, founded in 2004 by Sir Richard Branson, is a British space tourism company that aims to provide suborbital spaceflights to the public. The company uses its SpaceShipTwo, a winged spacecraft, launched from WhiteKnightTwo, a carrier aircraft, to take passengers to space. Virgin Galactic has been at the forefront of the commercial spaceflight revolution.

Famous Rockets

Virgin Galactic uses SpaceShipTwo, an air-launched suborbital spaceplane, for its space tourism missions. The spacecraft is launched from WhiteKnightTwo, a twin-fuselage carrier aircraft. The company's goal is to offer regular commercial flights for private passengers to the edge of space.

Satellites

Virgin Galactic does not focus on launching satellites like other space organizations. Instead, the company is dedicated to suborbital space tourism and has yet to launch satellite missions or participate in satellite deployment.

Involvement with ISS

Virgin Galactic is not involved with the International Space Station (ISS). Its primary focus is on suborbital space tourism, providing brief, but significant, flights for passengers to experience weightlessness and see the curvature of the Earth.

Major Missions

Virgin Galactic's major missions involve the successful flights of SpaceShipTwo, which has been tested multiple times. The company is also preparing for its commercial flight program, with the first passenger spaceflights expected soon. The goal is to offer a few minutes of weightlessness and stunning views of Earth from the edge of space.

Astronauts

Virgin Galactic has begun taking private individuals into space as astronauts. This includes high-profile flights such as that of Richard Branson, the company’s founder, who flew on SpaceShipTwo during its successful test flight in 2021, alongside other passengers. Sirisha Bandla, an Indian-American telugu aeronautical engineer and Vice President of Government Affairs at Virgin Galactic, who conducted a science experiment during the flight. Beth Moses, Virgin Galactic’s Chief Astronaut Instructor, who assessed the cabin experience for future passengers, were part of these milestone flights. The company is opening up space tourism to the general public, offering opportunities for civilians to become astronauts.

International Collaborations

Virgin Galactic has worked with various entities in the commercial space sector, collaborating with aerospace companies, government agencies, and private investors to develop its space tourism program. It has also worked closely with NASA for research into spaceflight technologies.

Internships & Student Opportunities

Virgin Galactic offers various internships and student programs for those interested in aerospace engineering, space tourism, and related fields. These opportunities provide hands-on experience in the rapidly growing commercial space industry.
