Firefly Aerospace

History & Overview

Firefly Aerospace is an American aerospace manufacturer and small satellite launch service provider founded in 2014. The company focuses on providing low-cost, reliable launch services for small payloads to low Earth orbit. Its rockets are designed to serve both government and commercial clients with rapid access to space.

Famous Rockets

Firefly Aerospace's primary rocket is the Alpha, a small satellite launch vehicle that is designed to provide frequent, low-cost access to space. The company is also working on a larger rocket, Beta, which will be capable of launching medium-sized payloads into low Earth orbit.

Satellites

Firefly Aerospace is focused on deploying small satellites into low Earth orbit for a variety of commercial, governmental, and scientific clients. These satellites are used for a range of applications including Earth observation, communications, and space science.

Involvement with ISS

Firefly Aerospace's rockets have been used to deploy payloads to the International Space Station (ISS) in support of scientific research and technological development. The company plays a key role in delivering small satellite payloads for ISS-related missions.

Major Missions

Firefly Aerospace has been involved in several missions to deploy small satellites for a variety of customers. Their missions support a broad range of applications, from communications to Earth observation.

Astronauts

Firefly Aerospace does not yet have a human spaceflight program, but the company is focused on small satellite launches and aims to develop rockets capable of delivering payloads to space for both government and commercial clients.

International Collaborations

Firefly Aerospace collaborates with various international clients and space agencies to provide launch services for small satellite missions. These collaborations support global space exploration and commercial space programs.

Internships & Student Opportunities

Firefly Aerospace offers internships and student opportunities for individuals interested in aerospace engineering, satellite technology, and rocket science. These programs give students the chance to work on real-world space missions and innovations.
