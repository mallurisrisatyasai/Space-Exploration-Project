Habitats and Laboratories in Orbit

üîπ What is a Space Station?

A space station is a large spacecraft in orbit around Earth or another celestial body, designed for humans to live and work in space for extended periods. They serve as laboratories for scientific research, technology testing, and international cooperation in space exploration.

üîπ Purpose of Space Stations

  • Scientific experiments in microgravity
  • Earth and space observation
  • Testing technologies for long-duration spaceflight
  • International collaboration and crew training

üîπ Key Features of Space Stations

  • Habitability modules for crew living
  • Scientific laboratories and equipment
  • Docking ports for spacecraft
  • Power generation (solar arrays)
  • Life support and environmental control systems

üîπ Challenges Faced by Space Stations

  • Maintaining life support and safety
  • Protection from space debris and radiation
  • Logistics of crew rotation and supply missions
  • Technical complexity and maintenance

üîπ Future of Space Stations

Plans include commercial space stations, lunar orbital platforms, and long-term habitats for missions to Mars and beyond, expanding human presence in space.

üîπ Fun Fact

The ISS travels around Earth approximately every 90 minutes, orbiting at about 28,000 kilometers per hour (17,500 miles per hour)!
