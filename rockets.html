ðŸ”¹ What is a Rocket?

A rocket is a vehicle propelled by the force of exhaust gases expelled from a combustion reaction. Based on Newton's Third Law, rockets work even in the vacuum of space.

ðŸ”¹ Types of Rockets

  • Launch Vehicles: Deliver payloads to space (e.g., Saturn V, Falcon 9, GSLV Mk III)
  • Sounding Rockets: Suborbital flights for scientific experiments (e.g., RH-200)
  • Missiles: Originated as weapons; some became launch systems
  • Boosters: Provide extra thrust during liftoff

ðŸ”¹ Types of Propulsion

  • Solid: Simple, instant ignition, non-throttleable
  • Liquid: Throttleable, restartable, more complex
  • Hybrid: Mix of solid fuel and liquid oxidizer

ðŸ”¹ Key Components of a Rocket

  • Propellant Tanks
  • Combustion Chamber
  • Nozzle
  • Payload Fairing
  • Guidance System

Modern rockets like Falcon 9 and New Shepard can land and fly again, reducing costs dramatically. Earlier examples include the Space Shuttle (partially reusable).
