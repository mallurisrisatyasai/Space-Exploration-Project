ISRO - Indian Space Research Organisation

History & Overview

ISRO was founded in 1969 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, often regarded as the father of India's space program. Its mission is to utilize space technology for national development, with a vision to become a global leader in space research and exploration. ISRO has achieved several milestones, such as launching satellites, conducting space missions, and sending India's first interplanetary mission, Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), to Mars in 2013.

Famous Rockets

ISRO has developed a range of rockets, including the PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle), which is known for its reliability and successful launches. The GSLV (Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle) is used for heavier payloads and has been pivotal in deploying communication satellites.

Satellites

ISRO has launched numerous satellites for various purposes, including communications, Earth observation, and navigation. Some notable satellites include: INSAT, GSAT, NavIC (Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System), and RISAT for radar imaging.

Space Missions

ISRO's space missions include the successful launch of Chandrayaan-1 (India's first lunar mission), the Chandrayaan-2 mission to the Moon, and the Mangalyaan mission to Mars. The recent Gaganyaan mission aims to send Indian astronauts into space for the first time.

Astronauts

Notable astronauts include Rakesh Sharma (India's first man in space) and Kalpana Chawla, who flew on the Space Shuttle Columbia. ISRO has also developed astronaut training programs and is planning to send Indian astronauts into space via the Gaganyaan mission.

Involvement with ISS

ISRO has yet to send astronauts to the International Space Station (ISS), but it collaborates with various space agencies like NASA, ESA, and Roscosmos for scientific research and technological exchange.

International Collaborations

ISRO has extensive international collaborations with agencies such as NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, CNES, and JAXA, covering various space missions, research, and satellite launches.
